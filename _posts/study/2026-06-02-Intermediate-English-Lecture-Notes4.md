## Renewable Energy 可再生能源

Renewable energy comes from sources that can be naturally replaced, such as sunlight, wind, water and geothermal heat. Unlike coal and oil, these sources do not depend on limited underground resources. As climate change becomes a global concern, more countries are investing in solar panels, wind farms and other clean energy projects.

The advantages of renewable energy are clear. It can reduce air pollution, lower carbon emissions and create new jobs in engineering, construction and maintenance. In some remote areas, small solar systems can provide electricity where large power stations are difficult to build. This makes energy supply more flexible and more equal.

However, renewable energy also faces challenges. Sunlight and wind are not always stable, so energy storage and smart power grids are important. The transition from traditional energy to clean energy requires money, technology and public support. It is a long process, but it is closely related to the future quality of human life.

## Plastic Pollution 塑料污染

Plastic is cheap, light and useful, so it appears almost everywhere in modern life. Bottles, bags, food packages and delivery boxes make shopping convenient. Yet the same convenience creates a serious environmental problem because many plastic products are used only once and then thrown away.

Plastic waste may enter rivers and oceans, where it can harm fish, birds and other animals. Over time, large plastic items break into tiny pieces called microplastics. These small pieces are difficult to collect and may enter the food chain. Scientists are still studying their possible influence on human health.

Reducing plastic pollution needs action from both governments and individuals. Factories can improve materials and packaging design. Shops can encourage reusable bags and containers. Ordinary people can refuse unnecessary plastic, sort waste correctly and choose durable products. Small choices, when repeated by millions of people, can produce a large effect.

## Artificial Intelligence 人工智能

Artificial intelligence, often shortened to AI, refers to computer systems that can perform tasks usually requiring human intelligence. These tasks may include recognizing speech, translating languages, recommending products, detecting diseases and driving vehicles. AI has already become part of daily life, even when people do not notice it.

The rapid development of AI brings many opportunities. It can help doctors read medical images, assist teachers in preparing learning materials and allow companies to analyze large amounts of data. For language learners, AI tools can correct grammar, explain difficult sentences and provide speaking practice at any time.

At the same time, AI raises important questions. People worry about privacy, unfair decisions, false information and changes in employment. Therefore, AI should be developed with clear rules and human responsibility. Technology is powerful, but it should serve human values instead of replacing them.

## Public Health 公共健康

Public health focuses on protecting and improving the health of entire communities. It is different from treating one patient after illness appears. Public health work includes vaccination, clean water, food safety, health education, disease prevention and emergency preparation.

A strong public health system can save many lives. For example, clear information helps people understand how diseases spread and how to protect themselves. Regular exercise, balanced diets and mental health support can reduce the risk of long-term illness. Good habits are easier to build when schools, workplaces and neighborhoods all provide healthy conditions.

Public health also depends on trust. During an emergency, people need accurate information instead of rumors. Doctors, scientists, officials and citizens should communicate honestly. When a society values both personal freedom and public responsibility, it becomes better prepared for future health challenges.

## Space Exploration 太空探索

Space exploration is the study of outer space through telescopes, satellites, robotic spacecraft and human missions. It satisfies human curiosity about the universe and helps us understand the Earth from a wider point of view. When people see our planet from space, they often realize how small and connected it is.

Space technology has practical benefits. Weather satellites help predict storms, navigation systems guide transportation and communication satellites connect distant areas. Research for space missions can also lead to improvements in materials, medicine and computer technology. In this sense, exploring space can support life on Earth.

Nevertheless, space exploration is expensive and risky. Some people argue that money should first be used to solve problems on Earth. A balanced view is necessary. Space programs should not become empty competition; they should encourage scientific cooperation and remind humanity to protect its only home.
